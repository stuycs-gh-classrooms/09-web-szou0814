Overview

Hamilton: An American Musical is a biographical musical inspired by the biography Alexander Hamilton by Ron Chernow. The musical was created by Lin-Manuel Miranda, the musical's playright, composer, and lyricist. After being composed for over 7 years, from 2008 to 2015, the musical finally premiered Off-Broadway on January 20, 2015 at The Public Theater in New York. Since its premiere, it has received numerous Tony, Grammy, Laurence Olivier, and Pulitzer Prize awards. It is now considered Broadway's most popular and second best selling show.

The soundtrack, drawing heavily from musical genres hip-hop, R&B, pop, soul, and traditional-style show tunes, narrates the life of founding father Alexander Hamilton. It begins with an introduction to Hamilton's immigrant, impoverished, and disadvantaged background and his arrival in New York. It details his involvement in the American Revolution, his marriage to Elizabeth Schuyler, and his career as a lawyer and Secretary of the Treasury and ends with his death by duel with Aaron Burr.

Hamilton consists of two separate acts, both narrated by Aaron Burr. Act I focuses on Alexander Hamilton's struggle to rise above his background and status through the revolution as George Washington's main aide-de-camp. With his arrival in New York, Hamilton first meets Aaron Burr, John Laurens, Marquis de Lafayette, and Hercules Mulligan. The five men quickly become close friends with their similar goals in the revolution and abolition of slavery. Hamilton then meets and enchants Elizabeth Schuyler, second daughter of wealthy Philip Schuyler, and gains the support and backing of her family. Through his achievements and efforts in the revolution, he rises to the position of Washington's right-hand man.
