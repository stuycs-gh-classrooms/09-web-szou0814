What is it?

Hamilton: An American Musical is a biographical musical inspired by the biography Alexander Hamilton by Ron Chernow. The musical was created by Lin-Manuel Miranda, the musical's playright, composer, and lyricist. After being composed for over 7 years, from 2008 to 2015, the musical finally premiered Off-Broadway on January 20, 2015 at The Public Theater in New York. Since its premiere, it has received numerous Tony, Grammy, Laurence Olivier, and Pulitzer Prize awards. It is now considered Broadway's most popular and second best selling show.

Hamilton consists of two separate acts, with a 15-minute intermission in between. The soundtrack, drawing heavily from musical genres hip-hop, R&B, pop, soul, and traditional-style show tunes, narrates the life of founding father Alexander Hamilton. It begins with an introduction to Hamilton's immigrant, impoverished, and disadvantaged background and his arrival in New York. It details his involvement in the American Revolution, his marriage to Elizabeth Schuyler, and his career as a lawyer and Secretary of the Treasury and ends with his death by duel with Aaron Burr.

Where can I watch?

  • Broadway Live at Richard Rodgers Theatre

    • Hamilton live on Broadway at Richard Rodgers Theatre has ticket costs starting at $115.73. The show is 2 hours and 45 minutes long, including one 15-minute intermission, and features adult language and themes---children under the age of 4 are not permitted. Showtimes include 7:00 PM on the weekdays and 1:00 PM or 7:00 PM on the weekends.

    ***Note: Hamilton live on Broadway is not performed by the original cast, which features Lin-Manuel Miranda, Leslie Odom Jr., and Phillipa Soo.

  • Disney+

    • Hamilton as played by the original cast, is filmed and available for viewing to Disney+ subscribers. Subscriptions start at $7.99/month.
